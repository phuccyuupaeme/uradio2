Why Adobe

While on the market to pick up a new hobby, I somehow got the bright idea to purchase a new camera FZ2500 by Panasonic and after doing so, I realized, this is only about 25% of the equipment I need. I spent countless hours searching for the right software program that would fit all my needs, sufficient enough to keep up with industry standards but not cost an arm and a leg if you know what I mean. Long story short, I chose Adobe Creative Cloud. The price was unbeatable (student discount price), the learning curve was very achievable, and last, Adobe's compatibility across platforms and other software programs is astounding.

Three reasons why I will be using Adobe now and in the distant future


*Ease of use
*Price
*Quality

Why FL Studio

Before I begin, let me say "Why not FL Studio"? No but seriously, FL Studio is the most user friendly, very compatible beat making software that I have ever come across and I have used many different ones, such as Reasons, Pro-Tools, and CoolEdit Pro just to name a few. I first began with FL Studio a few years back (I don’t remember the version, but at this time, 99% of plugins were free). If you’re a first timer, or looking to start a new hobby, or just want to have some fun, FL Studio is where it’s at, hands down. My current setup consists of an MPC5000, FLStudio (latest version) and a Roland F6. FLStudio is my main brain, the MPC is my drum machine and of course the Roland F6 is my keyboard for natural sounds.

Three reasons why I will NEVER stop using FLStudio


Not sure if this is true or not, but it seems as if every other music production software uses the basis of FLStudio, meaning if you can work FLStudio, you can work them all.

*Versitility
*Compatibility
